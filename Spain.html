SPAIN REPRESENTATION

Spain, increasingly vulnerable to climate change

Heat waves, extreme weather events, invasive species or intensification of certain diseases. Spain does not get rid of any of these consequences of climate change, now detailed in an extensive report prepared by Greenpeace on the occasion of the start of the United Nations Conference on Climate Change (COP24), held in Poland. The conclusion of the conservation organization is clear: we are one of the most vulnerable countries to climate change, so urgent and forceful action is necessary to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. To put black on white, Greenpeace has compiled in a report the different scientific evidence on climate change in our country, with images of extreme weather events and testimonies of experts in the field. "Global warming is the greatest danger we face," said José Luis García Orgenga, head of climate change at Greenpeace, at an informational breakfast that took place in Madrid on November 27. Ignoring scientific evidence is suicidal behavior. The time to act is now, there is no time to lose, ”he said
